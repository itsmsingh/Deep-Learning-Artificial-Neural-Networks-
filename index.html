What are Artificial Neural Networks?

Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs) are a subset of machine learning inspired by the structure and function of the human brain. They consist of interconnected nodes, or "neurons," organized in layersâ€”input, hidden, and output layers. Each neuron processes data and passes it forward, allowing the network to learn patterns and make predictions.

ANNs are widely used in applications such as image recognition, natural language processing, financial forecasting, and medical diagnostics. By adjusting internal parameters (weights and biases), ANNs improve their accuracy over time, making them a powerful tool for solving complex problems.

Real-World Applications of ANNs

1. Image Recognition

Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs) are widely used in image recognition tasks, such as facial recognition in smartphones, medical imaging for disease detection, and self-driving cars for object detection. For example, ANNs can analyze thousands of medical images to identify patterns indicative of diseases like cancer, enabling early diagnosis and treatment.

2. Natural Language Processing (NLP)

ANNs power many NLP applications, such as chatbots, language translation, and sentiment analysis. For instance, chatbots like ChatGPT use ANNs to understand and generate human-like text, making them useful for customer support, virtual assistants, and more.
